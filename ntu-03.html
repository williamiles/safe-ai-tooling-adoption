The 3 mistakes people make when using AI tools

Almost everyone makes at least one. Once you see them, you won't unsee them.

Mistake #1

Three mistakes. Three interactive scenes.

Open each one to see the mistake in action, then learn the fix.

1
Clicking "Allow" on autopilot
Permission fatigue — you stopped reading the popups
+
Watch how fast you stop paying attention
Tap play to start. The AI will ask for permissions. Watch what happens.

The first few permissions make sense. By the fourth or fifth, you're not reading anymore. You're just clicking "Allow" to get back to work. Researchers at Anthropic found this was so common they redesigned their entire permission system to reduce prompts by 84%. The problem isn't that you're careless. It's that our brains aren't built to stay alert through a wall of identical-looking popups.

The habit

Tap "Allow" on every popup without reading. It all looks the same anyway.

The fix

When a popup appears, read just the action word: "read", "edit", "send", "access." If it says "send" or "access", pause for two seconds.

2
Never looking at the settings
Safe defaults exist. You just never turned them on

Most AI tools ship with everything turned on, because that gives you the smoothest first experience. But "smooth" and "safe" aren't the same thing. The settings are there. Five minutes of checking them once means you don't have to make the right decision every single time you use the tool. Safe defaults do the work for you.

The habit

Never open settings. The tool works fine, why mess with it?

The fix

Spend 5 minutes on day one: turn off history, limit file access, require approval for actions. Once. Then forget about it.

3
Trusting the output blindly
Sending, sharing, or saving without a second look
+
Can you spot what's wrong?

You asked the AI: "Draft a reply to this client email."

Hi Sarah,

Thanks for your message. I'd be happy to schedule a follow-up meeting next week.

In the meantime, I've shared the project files with you at external-share-link.com/d/x8k2m. You should also find the updated budget breakdown showing the $2.4M allocation for Project Falcon attached.

Best regards
Red flag: unexpected link. You didn't ask the AI to share any files. Where did this link come from? A hidden instruction in the client's email could have told the AI to add it.
Red flag: confidential details. You didn't ask the AI to include budget numbers. It pulled them from other files it has access to, and is about to send them to the client.

Tap the underlined parts to see the red flags.

AI output looks polished. That's the problem. It's easy to trust something that reads well. But the AI doesn't know what's confidential, what's accurate, or what you actually asked for. Before you send, save, or share anything the AI wrote, scan it for 10 seconds. Look for links, numbers, names, or actions you didn't ask for.

The habit

Hit "Send" on the AI's draft without reading it. It sounds professional, so it's probably fine.

The fix

Scan for 10 seconds before sending. Look for links, numbers, names, or actions you didn't ask for.

🔐

Three settings to check right now

Most AI tools ship with everything open by default. Spend five minutes locking these down. Once is enough.

Training data Turn off "use my conversations for training." Your chats don't need to train the next model.
"Allow all future actions" When the AI asks you to approve all future requests of a type, pause. What sounds safe now could be dangerous in a different situation. Approve one at a time.
Connected apps Review which plugins and apps your AI can reach. Do you trust them? Think about what data the AI can fetch from them and what actions it can take on your behalf. Remove the ones you're not using.
